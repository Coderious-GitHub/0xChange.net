What is 0xChange.net?

0xChange.net is a Decentralized Exchange (DEX) to trade ERC-20 Tokens on the Ethereum Blockchain. The exchange is based on a Smart Contract that manages the order book and the transactions.

0xChange.net is one of the only DEX providing a full on-chain matching. What does it mean? It means that you can just place your order and the exchange will do the rest by finding one or more counterparties.

It makes the experience much closer to the one you can enjoy on a Centralized exchange. In addition, trading is more efficient as many transactions can be executed in one block. It goes faster and gas cost is lower for large orders (see cost section below).

How does it work?

To use the exchange, you will need a Metamask account (www.metamask.io). Once connected, just place your buy or sell order we take care of the rest.

A small fee of 0.25% is charged on the ETH payment to the seller of token, no fees on the buyer. So if you want to buy 100 xTokens you will receive exactly 100 xTokens. Gas cost is estimated as follows:

  • New Order: ~250-300k
  • +50k per trade
  • Example: if you place an order to buy 100 xTokens and your order needs 5 counterparties to be fulfilled, the gas cost is estimated to 500k (250k + 5x50k). This would be the equivalent of placing 5 distinct orders on a standard DEX without automated matching.

Exchange Rules

Orders

Orders are managed as follows:

  • The exchange accepts limit and market orders
  • Orders with a limit of zero are considered market orders.
  • To place a buy order at the market, an ETH amount must be sent with the order
  • An order is deemed executed when the token amount requested is reached or when the ETH left on an order is smaller than 0.000000001 ETH.
  • New orders are placed in the order book and sorted first by price limit then by block.nr

Price

Each time a trader places an order, she initates the matching mechanism:

  • Matching between two market orders is executed at the lowest of the last paid price or the best ask price.
  • Matching between two limit orders depends on the direction of the trade:
    • If a sell order initiates the matching, the bid price is used
    • If a buy order initiates the matching, the ask price is used
